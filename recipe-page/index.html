Cardboard Box Casserole

Background

This recipe has been passed down for generations. The culinary process is said to have originated in the bustling kitchens of a royal palace, where a fussy royal cat once rejected a solid-gold food bowl in favor of the plain, discarded box it came in. The recipe became a courtly delicacy, known for its rustic charm and its ability to turn even the most regal feline into a blissful lump. Its popularity spread, proving that sometimes the packaging is more appetizing than the contents.

Ingredients

  • A Cat
  • One Cardboard box, ideally slightly too small for the cat
  • Packing Peanuts or Crumpled Paper for added texture (optional)

Instructions

  1. Place the empty box on the floor.
  2. Observe as the cat immediatelyt investigates the box with intense curiosity.
  3. The cat will then attempt to squeeze itself into the box, regardless of the size difference.
  4. Allow the cat to settle in its new cardboard dwelling, occassionally peeking in to check on the 'casserole'.
  5. Serve by leaving the cat to enjoy its cozy, if slightly cramped, space.
